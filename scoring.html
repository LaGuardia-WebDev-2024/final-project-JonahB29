Scoring

There are 2 parts to a gymnastics score, Difficulty and Execution. Difficulty is as it sounds like, how difficult the exercise is. Execution is how well the exercise is competed. The sum of these two scores determine the final score. The Code of Points outines the rules and scoring procedures for gymnastics and is updated every 4 years for the next Olympic quadrennium.

Difficulty Execution
  • The first component of a gymnastics score is the top 8 most difficult elements in the routine
  • Each element is assigned a value based on its difficulty.
    • A=0.1 (least difficult elements)
    • B=0.2
    • C=0.3
    • D=0.4
    • E=0.5
    • F=0.6
    • G=0.7
    • H=0.8
    • I=0.9
    • J=1.0 (most difficult element, only one exists)
  • The second component of a gymnastics score is connection value abbreviated as CV. This is a bonus of either 0.1 or 0.2 for linking difficult elements together.
  • The third component of a gymnastics score is a 0.5 for fullfilling a composition requirent for a routine. Men have 3 composition requirements and a dismount requirement, while women have 4 composition requirements and no dismount requirement.
  • The execution score starts from a 10.0 and deductions are taken for errors in the routine
  • Deductions range from 0.1 tenth of a point to a full point
    • 0.1 is for a small error
    • 0.3 is for a medium error
    • 0.5 is for a major error
    • 1.0 is taken for a fall
  • 0.1 - 0.5 can be taken for Neutral deductions ie.
    • stepping out of bounds
    • not finishing within the required time limit for an exercise
    • not saluting the judges
    • coach spotting during the routine
